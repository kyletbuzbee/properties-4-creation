Privacy Policy

How we protect and use your personal information

Last Updated: December 14, 2025

Overview

Properties 4 Creations ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our website, apply for housing, or interact with our services.

We are a veteran-owned housing provider specializing in premium, renovated homes for veterans and families in East Texas. We comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations, including the Fair Housing Act and Texas privacy laws.

Information We Collect

Personal Information

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact Information: Name, phone number, email address, mailing address
  • Demographic Information: Date of birth, household size, income information
  • Housing Information: Housing assistance program participation, voucher details
  • Military Service Information: Branch of service, discharge status (for veteran applicants)
  • Housing Preferences: Preferred property types, accessibility needs, move-in timeline
  • Communication Records: Messages, questions, and feedback you send to us

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect:

  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent on pages, click patterns
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, device type
  • Analytics: Google Analytics data for website improvement

How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

Housing Services

  • Process housing applications and determine eligibility
  • Verify income and voucher information with housing authorities
  • Match applicants with suitable properties
  • Manage lease agreements and property assignments
  • Coordinate move-in processes and property showings

Communication

  • Respond to inquiries and provide customer support
  • Send important updates about applications and properties
  • Provide information about housing programs and resources
  • Share transparency reports and property information

Legal Compliance

  • Comply with Fair Housing laws and anti-discrimination requirements
  • Meet reporting requirements for housing programs
  • Maintain accurate records for landlord-tenant relationships
  • Protect against fraud and ensure tenant safety

Website Improvement

  • Analyze website usage to improve user experience
  • Debug technical issues and ensure site functionality
  • Personalize content based on housing interests

Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share information only in the following circumstances:

Housing Program Verification

  • HUD and VA: Verify voucher status and eligibility with HUD-VASH coordinators or VA representatives
  • Public Housing Authorities: Confirm Section 8 voucher validity and compliance
  • Background Check Providers: Conduct tenant screening for safety and compliance

Legal Requirements

  • Respond to subpoenas, court orders, or legal requirements
  • Report suspected fraud, criminal activity, or safety threats
  • Comply with government housing program audits and reporting

Service Providers

  • Property management software providers (with data protection agreements)
  • Professional services (legal, accounting) under confidentiality agreements
  • Website hosting and maintenance providers

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical, administrative, and physical security measures to protect your personal information:

Technical Protections

  • SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • Secure data storage with access controls
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Multi-factor authentication for staff access

Administrative Protections

  • Limited access to personal information on need-to-know basis
  • Staff training on privacy and security procedures
  • Regular security awareness programs
  • Incident response and breach notification procedures

Physical Protections

  • Secure office facilities with access controls
  • Document storage in locked cabinets
  • Secure disposal of physical records

Your Rights and Choices

Access and Correction

You have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal information we maintain
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Receive a copy of your information in a portable format

Communication Preferences

You may:

  • Opt out of marketing communications (though we rarely send these)
  • Choose how you wish to be contacted for application updates
  • Update your contact information at any time

Data Deletion

You can request deletion of your information, though we may retain certain information as required by law or for legitimate business purposes, such as:

  • Completed lease records (for legal compliance)
  • Financial records (for tax and accounting purposes)
  • Communications about safety or legal matters

Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies:

Essential Cookies

  • Remember your session and security preferences
  • Enable website functionality and navigation
  • Process applications and forms

Analytics Cookies

  • Google Analytics to understand website usage
  • Help improve user experience
  • Identify technical issues

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings, though disabling essential cookies may affect website functionality.

Children's Privacy

Our services are intended for adults 18 years and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child, we will delete it immediately.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will:

  • Post the updated policy on our website
  • Update the "Last Updated" date above
  • Notify you of significant changes via email or website notice
  • Continue to respect the privacy choices you've made

Complaints and Dispute Resolution

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated:

  • Contact us directly first for resolution
  • File a complaint with the Texas Attorney General's Office
  • Report housing discrimination to HUD (fairhousing@HUD.gov)
  • Contact appropriate state or federal regulatory agencies
